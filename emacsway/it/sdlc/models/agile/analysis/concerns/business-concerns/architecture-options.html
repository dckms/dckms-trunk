An option is therefore a way to defer a decision: instead of deciding to buy or sell a stock today, you have the right to make that decision in the future, at a known price.

Following Black-Scholes, options whose strike price is higher than the stock‚Äôs current price still have value. So it‚Äôs OK if exercising the (architecture) option in the future has a slightly higher price than today.
